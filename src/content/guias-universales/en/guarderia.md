## What options exist for babies and young children

Before the mandatory schooling age (which starts with Kindergarten,
usually around age 4 depending on the canton), several childcare
options exist:

- **Kita / crèche / asilo nido** (institutional daycare): groups of
  children roughly 3 months to 4 years old, with professional childcare
  staff. This is the most sought-after option and has the longest
  waiting lists.
- **Tagesfamilie / accueil familial / famiglia diurna** (family
  daycare): a certified childminder looks after a few children in their
  own home. Usually more flexible on hours and cheaper than
  institutional daycare.
- **Private nanny/babysitter**: no specific cantonal regulation, a
  direct arrangement between families.

## Register as early as possible — demand is very high

In cities like Zurich, Geneva, or Basel, waiting lists for the most
sought-after daycares can run many months, sometimes over a year.
Practical tips:

- Register at **several daycares at once** as soon as you know you're
  moving or will need a spot — even before you have an exact start date
  confirmed.
- Also check your commune's Tagesfamilie options, which sometimes have
  shorter waiting lists than institutional daycares.
- Your commune's website usually has the official list of subsidized
  daycares; kibesuisse.ch has a nationwide directory.

## How much it costs

Prices vary a lot by canton, by commune, and in many places **based on
your household income** (lower subsidized rates for lower incomes). As
a very rough reference, a full day of daycare can range from around
CHF 50 to 150, but the real range depends entirely on where you live —
always check the exact rate with the commune or the specific daycare.

## Kindergarten: the next step

Kindergarten is public, free, and mandatory in most cantons from around
age 4, for 1 to 2 years before starting primary school. Enrolment is
handled through your commune's school system, usually with advance
notice from the schooling office as your child approaches the right
age.

## Important notice

Age requirements, exact fees, and the subsidy system vary by canton
and commune, and change over time. Always confirm the details with
your commune's office or the specific daycare before planning your
return to work.
