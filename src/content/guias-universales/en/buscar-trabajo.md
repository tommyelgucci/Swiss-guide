## Portals by job type

- **General**: jobs.ch, indeed.ch, jobup.ch
- **Public sector / administration**: each canton publishes its own
  vacancies on its own website
- **Banking/finance**: LinkedIn tends to be more effective than generic
  portals
- **Tech/IT profiles**: besides LinkedIn, many companies post directly on
  their own careers page — it's worth checking target companies one by one

## Unsolicited applications (Initiativbewerbung/candidature spontanée)

In Switzerland, it's very normal to write directly to a company you're
interested in even if it hasn't posted an opening. A typical application
dossier includes:

1. Cover letter (1 page, tailored to that specific company)
2. CV (ideally 2 pages, with a photo — unlike the US/UK, a photo on the CV
   is still standard practice in Switzerland)
3. Copies of diplomas and previous work certificates
   (Arbeitszeugnis/certificat de travail)

## The work certificate (Arbeitszeugnis)

Every Swiss employer is legally required to issue this document when an
employment relationship ends, written in a coded style: certain
seemingly-neutral phrases are actually negative signals to those who know
how to read them. If you work in Switzerland, always keep your previous
work certificates — you'll be asked for them in every future application.

## Language level

While many international companies operate in English, the local cantonal
language (German, French, or Italian) is often decisive outside Zurich,
Geneva, or Basel, even for technical hiring processes.
