## Opening an account

Banks typically ask for:

- Passport or ID card
- Registration confirmation from your commune (or residence permit)
- Employment contract

Banks like UBS, PostFinance, Raiffeisen, or cantonal banks (e.g. ZKB, BCV)
are common choices. Digital banks like Neon or Yuh are also available,
faster to open but with fewer physical branches.

## The QR-bill (QR-Rechnung)

Switzerland has its own billing system: instead of a simple IBAN transfer,
bills (rent, insurance, taxes) usually arrive with a QR code that you scan
from your banking app to pay without manually entering the details. It's
been the standard since 2022 and replaced the old orange/red payment slip
system (still occasionally found at older businesses).

## Twint

A mobile payment app widely used in Switzerland, for both peer-to-peer
payments (splitting a dinner bill) and in-store purchases. It's linked to
your bank account and nearly universal — worth setting up as soon as you
arrive.
