## There's no federal minimum wage

Unlike most European countries, Switzerland has no minimum wage at the
federal level (some cantons have introduced one, such as Geneva,
Neuchâtel, or Jura). Salaries are negotiated individually or follow
sector-wide collective agreements (CCT/GAV) if your sector has one.

## Key points to check in your contract

- **Notice period** (délai de congé/Kündigungsfrist): usually 1 month
  during the first year, extending afterward. Check whether your contract
  improves on or matches the legal minimum.
- **Vacation**: the legal minimum is 4 weeks a year (5 if you're under 20).
  Many companies offer more.
- **Overtime** (heures supplémentaires/Überstunden): check whether it's
  compensated with pay, time off, or already included in your salary.
- **Accident insurance** (LAA/UVG): mandatory; usually the employer pays
  for occupational accidents and the employee for non-occupational ones
  (deducted from payroll).
- **13th salary**: very common in Switzerland (an extra month's pay in
  December), but not legally required unless your contract or CCT
  specifies it — confirm before signing.

## Probation period

Typically 1 to 3 months, during which the notice period is reduced to 7
calendar days for either party.
