## The rental dossier

In Switzerland, it's not enough for the landlord to like you: you're
competing against dozens of applicants with a standardized file. Prepare it
before you start searching:

- **Debt collection extract** (Betreibungsauszug / extrait des poursuites /
  estratto delle esecuzioni) — requested from your commune's debt collection
  office (Betreibungsamt). Costs 15-20 CHF and proves you have no unpaid
  debts.
- **Last 3 payslips**, or employment contract if you've just arrived
- **Copy of passport/ID** and residence permit (or registration confirmation
  if you don't have it yet)
- **Reference letter** from a previous landlord (optional, but helps)
- **Application form** (Bewerbungsformular) — many agencies have their own
  on their website

## Where to search

The most-used portals are Homegate, ImmoScout24 and Comparis. For
high-demand apartments, many listings receive hundreds of applications
within hours — you need to react fast, and sometimes attend group viewings
(Besichtigung/visite).

## The deposit (Kaution/dépôt de garantie)

Usually equal to 2-3 months' rent, held in a blocked bank account in your
name (never handed to the landlord in cash). It's released at the end of
the lease if there's no unpaid damage.
