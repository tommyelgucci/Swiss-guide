## What AHV is

AHV/AVS (Alters- und Hinterlassenenversicherung / old-age and
survivors' insurance) is the **first pillar** of the Swiss pension
system: mandatory insurance that funds retirement pensions, as well as
survivor benefits (widowhood, orphanhood) and disability (via the
associated AI/IV insurance). It's mandatory for practically everyone
who works or lives in Switzerland from age 18.

## Who contributes and how much

- **Employees**: your employer withholds your share of the
  contribution directly from your payslip and pays the other half. The
  total is a bit over 8% of gross salary, split equally between you and
  your employer — the exact rate is adjusted periodically.
- **Self-employed**: you pay the full contribution yourself (no
  employer half), calculated on your income, with a reduced scale for
  lower incomes.
- **Not working** (e.g., a spouse without paid employment): you may
  still need to pay a minimum annual contribution to avoid gaps.

## Why avoiding "gaps" matters

Your future pension depends on how many full years you've contributed.
Years without contributions (gaps) reduce your final retirement
pension, even once you reach retirement age. You can request your
individual account statement from your cantonal compensation office to
check your contribution history.

## What happens to your contributions if you leave Switzerland

This depends on whether your destination country has a **social
security agreement** with Switzerland:

- **With an agreement** (all EU/EFTA countries and several third
  countries, such as the US, Canada, and others with a bilateral
  agreement): your years contributed in Switzerland count toward your
  future pension — you can't request a refund, but you keep the right
  to receive your Swiss pension once you reach retirement age, wherever
  you're living by then. Agreements usually let you combine
  (totalize) years contributed in both countries to meet minimum
  requirements.
- **Without an agreement**: you can request a **full cash refund**
  (Barauszahlung) of your first-pillar contributions, generally
  starting one year after you stop being insured in Switzerland. This
  refund only covers AHV — money accumulated in the second pillar
  (BVG/LPP, your occupational pension) is handled separately and has
  its own exit rules.

## Important notice

Contribution rates, the list of countries with an agreement, and the
exact procedures change over time. Before making decisions about your
retirement or your departure from Switzerland, confirm your case at
ahv-iv.ch or with your cantonal compensation office.
