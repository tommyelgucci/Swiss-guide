## The main permit types

- **Permit L** — short-term. For contracts under 12 months. Tied to the
  length of your employment contract.
- **Permit B** — residence. For contracts of 12 months or more, or
  open-ended. Renewed annually (EU/EFTA citizens) or under stricter
  conditions (third-country nationals).
- **Permit C** — settlement. Granted after a number of years of continuous
  residence (varies by nationality and bilateral agreement: 5 or 10 years).
  Gives nearly the same rights as a Swiss citizen, except voting at the
  federal level.
- **Permit G** — cross-border commuter. For those who work in Switzerland
  but live in a neighboring country (France, Germany, Italy, Austria,
  Liechtenstein) and return home at least once a week.

## Registering on arrival

You must register at the town hall (Gemeinde/Commune/Comune) of your place
of residence within **14 days** of arrival. You'll need:

- Passport or ID card
- Employment contract
- Rental contract or proof of accommodation
- Passport-style photos (sometimes taken on-site)

## Can I work before receiving the physical card?

Yes. In most cantons, the registration confirmation (sometimes called an
"attestation" or "Anmeldebestätigung") is enough to start working while you
wait for the physical card, which can take several weeks to arrive by mail.
