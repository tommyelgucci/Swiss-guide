## The family doctor (Hausarzt/médecin de famille)

Under many insurance models (HMO, family doctor model) you're required to
see your family doctor first before a specialist, except for real
emergencies or gynaecology/paediatrics. Choosing this model lowers your
monthly premium in exchange for that restriction. With "free choice"
models you can skip this step, but you pay more each month.

## Emergency room vs. phone triage

Going straight to the ER (Notfall/urgences) of a hospital without calling
first can mean an extra fee on your bill, depending on your insurer. The
usual approach is to call first:

- **144** — ambulance/life-threatening emergency
- Your canton's **medical phone triage service** (Medgate, Medi24, or
  another depending on your insurer) — they tell you whether you need the
  ER or a next-day appointment is enough

## Pharmacies (Apotheke/pharmacie)

For minor ailments, the pharmacy is the first filter: they can recommend
over-the-counter medication and advise you without an appointment. Many
cities have a rotating on-call pharmacy (Notfallapotheke) outside normal
hours — it's posted on the door of any nearby pharmacy.

## Billing and reimbursement

In the Swiss system you typically pay first (consultation, pharmacy,
hospital) and then claim reimbursement from your insurer, after your
annual deductible (franchise) and the 10% co-payment (Selbstbehalt) up to
an annual cap. Keep all invoices and send them to your insurer — many
have an app to scan them directly.
