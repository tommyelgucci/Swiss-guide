## Half-Fare (Halbtax)

The most popular pass: costs around CHF 190-200 a year and gives a 50%
discount on most train, bus, tram, and boat tickets nationwide. It pays
for itself quickly if you travel with any regularity.

## GA (Generalabonnement / abonnement général)

Unlimited travel pass across the entire Swiss public network (trains,
buses, trams, and even some boats). It's expensive (several thousand CHF
a year), but worthwhile if you commute long distances daily or travel a
lot for work.

## Regional/cantonal passes

Each local transport network (ZVV in Zurich, TPG in Geneva, TL in
Lausanne...) has its own monthly/annual passes for travel within its zone,
usually cheaper than the GA if you don't leave your region.

## SBB app

The official Swiss railways app (SBB/CFF/FFS) lets you buy tickets, check
timetables, and in some cases even use the app itself as your "ticket"
via app-based fare control (Easy Ride). It's the go-to tool for getting
around the country.
