## What it is

Quellensteuer (impôt à la source / imposta alla fonte) is the system under
which, if you hold a B or L permit (and in some cases a recent C permit),
your employer deducts income tax directly from your payslip each month and
forwards it to the tax authority. You don't file a traditional annual
return, with some exceptions.

## When you must still file a return

- If your gross annual income exceeds a threshold (varies by canton,
  typically between CHF 120,000 and 150,000)
- If you have income outside your payslip (rental income, investments,
  self-employment)
- If you obtain a C permit, or marry a Swiss citizen or C-permit holder
- If you want to claim specific deductions (training costs, alimony,
  pillar 3a contributions) — you can request a "correction"
  (Tarifkorrektur) without filing a full return

## Differences by canton

Each canton sets its own rates and brackets, so the same payslip can result
in a different withholding depending on where you live, not just where you
work. This often causes surprises when changing canton of residence without
changing jobs.
