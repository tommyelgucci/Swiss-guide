## A decentralized system

There's no single ministry of education: each of the 26 cantons regulates
its own school system (calendar, curriculum, language of instruction),
and within the canton, day-to-day management depends on the municipality
(commune) where you live. This means the process and deadlines change
depending on where you register.

## How to enroll a child

1. Register your residence with your municipality (Anmeldung/annonce) —
   this mandatory step comes first.
2. Contact your municipality's school administration
   (Schulverwaltung/direction des écoles), usually via its website.
3. You're assigned the public school that matches your address — you
   don't freely choose a school in the public system, it's zone-based.
4. Provide documentation: passport/permit, residence registration
   certificate, and sometimes previous school records if the child was
   already enrolled elsewhere.

## Language of instruction

Switzerland has no single school language: teaching is in German, French,
or Italian depending on the linguistic region you live in (Romansh only
in specific areas of Graubünden). If your child doesn't speak the local
language, most municipalities offer intensive support classes
(integration classes/DaZ, classes d'accueil) during the first year.

## Compulsory schooling and schedules

Compulsory schooling typically starts at age 4-5 with
Kindergarten/école enfantine and lasts until age 15-16. Many cantons
offer paid school lunch (cantine) and after-school childcare
(structures d'accueil parascolaire), useful if both parents work —
demand usually exceeds available spots, so it's worth signing up early.
